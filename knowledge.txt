### SKYPAY COMPANY OVERVIEW
Skybridge Payment, Inc. (doing business as SKYPAY) is a Philippines-based fintech company specializing in payment gateway services and digital payment solutions[cite: 415, 436].
The company is officially licensed by the Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP) as an Operator of Payment System (OPS)[cite: 416, 430].
SkyPay is a legitimate SEC-registered and secured fintech firm operated by Skybridge Payments Inc[cite: 432, 446, 447].
SkyPay is NOT a loaning or lending company and does not provide credit, financing services, or directly extend financial credit to customers[cite: 402, 419, 444].
The company operates as a B2B payment infrastructure provider and a payment bridge connecting merchants, financial institutions, lenders, billers, and enterprise platforms[cite: 401, 408, 417].

### CORE SERVICES & IDENTITY
SkyPay functions as a backend payment infrastructure provider enabling organizations to process payments, collections, and billing transactions[cite: 403, 424].
Services include Over-the-Counter (OTC) and digital payment collections, cash payouts, and bills payment for over 200 biller partners[cite: 404, 438].
Value-added services include Buy Load, Top Up, and Cash In[cite: 438].
Disbursement channels supported: InstaPay, PESONet, and various e-wallets like GCash, PayMaya, and Coins.ph[cite: 439].
SkyPay provides API-based integrations for transaction posting, payment validation, and reference number verification[cite: 405, 425].

### TYPICAL PAYMENT FLOW & USAGE
1. Merchant or lender generates a payment reference number[cite: 410, 427].
2. Customer pays through supported channels such as 7-Eleven, GCash, PayMaya, QR Ph, or Banks[cite: 411, 428].
3. SkyPay processes, routes, and posts the transaction to the partner system[cite: 412, 429].

### IMPORTANT NOTE ON SUDDEN FUNDS
If a user receives money suddenly in their account and SkyPay is mentioned, it is NOT money given by SkyPay[cite: 420, 444]. 
SkyPay is only the technology bridge used to facilitate the transfer[cite: 401, 418]. 
The funds actually originate from a third-party loaning app or company that the user previously clicked on or used.
SkyPay does not issue loans and does not have the authority to "give" money to individuals directly[cite: 402, 414].
